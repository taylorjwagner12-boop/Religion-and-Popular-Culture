How music, film, celebrity culture, and modern media continue shaping morality, identity, and belonging in modern society.

The Big Question

Before this course, I mostly thought of religion as something connected to churches, prayer, and organized belief systems. What surprised me was how often religious ideas appeared in places I was not expecting, especially in films, music, celebrity culture, and online communities. Even when people are not consciously thinking about religion, it continues to influence how we define morality, identity, purpose, and belonging.

Artists, films, and celebrities often take on roles that resemble traditional religious structures. Concerts function like rituals, fandoms create communities around shared identity, and films explore sacrifice, redemption, transcendence, and apocalypse in ways that mirror religious storytelling.

This website explores how religion never truly disappeared from modern culture. Instead, it evolved into new forms that continue shaping the way people think, connect, and understand the world around them.

About This Project

Throughout this course, I became interested in how religion continues influencing modern culture even outside traditional religious spaces. At first, I thought religion was primarily connected to organized institutions and formal beliefs. However, the more examples we discussed in class, the more I noticed religious themes appearing in films, music, celebrity culture, sports, fandoms, and even everyday habits. Looking at examples such as I Am Legend, hip hop artists like Kendrick Lamar and Kanye West, celebrity communities, and personal experiences with live music helped me realize that people continue searching for meaning, identity, community, and belonging in many of the same ways they always have. This website explores how religion has adapted to modern culture rather than disappeared from it.

🔗 ✉️

Why This Matters

Modern culture may appear secular on the surface, but many of the same questions religion has always asked are still underneath it:

What gives life meaning?

Who do we follow?

What do we believe in?

How do we find belonging?

By recognizing how religion has evolved rather than disappeared, we gain insight into what drives human connection, moral behavior, and the stories we choose to tell about ourselves. The goal is not to argue that pop culture is religion, but to show how many of the same human needs for meaning, identity, and community continue to appear in new forms.

What Changed My Thinking?

At the beginning of the quarter, I viewed religion primarily as a set of beliefs and practices tied to organized institutions. By the end of the course, I began to see religion as something broader. Many of the same needs that religion has traditionally fulfilled, including meaning, identity, morality, community, and belonging, continue to appear throughout popular culture. This shift in perspective is what shaped the analysis throughout this website.
