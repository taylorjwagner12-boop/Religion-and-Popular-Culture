The Big Question

Most people think of religion as something tied to churches, prayer, or organized belief systems. But its influence shows up everywhere— in the films we watch, the music we listen to, and the celebrities we follow. Religion shapes our values, behaviors, and ideas about right and wrong. Even when we're not directly thinking about it, religion influences how we define morality, identity, and what we consider acceptable.

The question isn't whether religion matters in popular culture. It's how deeply it shapes us, often without us realizing it.

About This Project

This project explores how religion intersects with popular culture—from blockbuster films to chart-topping music, from celebrity personas to viral trends. Religion isn't confined to places of worship; it's woven into the stories we watch, the music we listen to, and the figures we follow. By examining specific examples, we discover what these intersections reveal about society, belief, morality, and human meaning-making.

🔗 ✉️

Key Themes

Science vs. Faith

When logic alone fails to explain suffering, does religion offer answers that science cannot?

Meaning-Making

Religion and culture constantly reshape each other, creating new ways for people to find purpose and identity.

Hidden Influence

Religious concepts influence everyday life—from dietary rules to moral judgments—often without people realizing it.

Modern Spirituality

Concerts, fan communities, and celebrity culture now function as spaces for spiritual experience and shared meaning.
